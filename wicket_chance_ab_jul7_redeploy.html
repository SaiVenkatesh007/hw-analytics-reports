Key findings

Narrative summary

Median wickets 2→3 over a full week: Holds in every bracket — e.g. 0–50k median 2→3, mean 2.57→3.33 (+30%). Not a one-day spike.
Scores fall 3–8%; innings flat: Mean scores −7.7% (0–50k) to −2.8% (100k+). Balls faced ~26; WR dip modest (0–50k 48.8→43.5, −5.3pp).
Mild shot adaptation: Zeros/ones up ~+0.5–1.9pp; fours/sixes down ~−0.5–1.6pp. Strongest below 75k.
Same-day continuation neutral: Match-level next-match retention Base 83.68% vs Exp 83.74% (+0.06pp).
Next-day retention underpowered: Among ≥5-match team-days, Exp is only ~2.7k vs ~95k Base. Too thin for a proper D+1 read at ~20% Exp — scale to 50% and re-read.

Mild risk reallocation: Shot mix shifts ~1–2pp — zeros/ones up, fours/sixes down. Players respond slightly but do not overhaul shot selection.
