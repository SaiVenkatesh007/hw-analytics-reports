Key findings

What the baseline tells us

Overall health is solid: Close matches (~55%) outnumber blowouts (~28%) ~2:1. Median power ratio at 1.00 and ~52% user WR at the 0.95–1.0 parity band suggest MM intent is fair on average.
High-frequency players are the priority segment: 15+/day players see 46.7% close and 35.5% one-sided (vs 58.1% / 25.9% for casual). Only 1,325 users, but they play the most matches per user and are most sensitive to MM quality.
Mid-competitive ranks are the soft spot: Accomplished (52.1% close, 31.0% blowout) and Supreme+ (50.1%, 32.8%) underperform Rookie/Challenger. Masterful recovers at 58.4% close — tighter, higher-power pool.
Power tails break above 75k: At parity band WR is ~50–53%, but when matched outside 0.95–1.0 at 75k–100k+, outcomes become extreme (87% WR as favourite, 13% as underdog).
Recalibrate benchmarks: The original 30–40% close-match target does not match this margin definition. Use ~55% / ~28% as the Phase 1 baseline until a longer window confirms.

Methodology

Scope, filters, and metric definitions

Data scope

    Metric definitions

    • Close match: Batting first → margin ≤5 runs; bowling first → ≤3 balls remaining (18-ball format for Rookie 1–2, else 30).
    • One-sided match: Batting first → margin ≥15 runs; bowling first → ≥12 balls remaining.
    • Match-level rate: % of unique matches flagged close / one-sided.
    • Per-user rate: Each user's personal close % within a segment, then averaged across users (≥4 matches in segment).
    • Power ratio: opp_team_internal_strength / team_internal_strength from calibratedTotalInternalStrength.
    • Activity tier: match_count / active_days in window — Casual ≤5, Regular 6–15, High-freq 15+.

    Caveats

    • 5-day window only — extend to 4–8 weeks before locking operational targets.
    • ~96K rows missing power bracket (null calibrated strength) excluded from power-ratio analysis.
    • Small-n cells (e.g. cross-rank × high power) should not drive product decisions alone.
