Key findings

What exposure shows

Training breadth flat: unique batsmen trained median stays at 3 before and after Retire unlock. No roster expansion signal.
Training intensity rises: median level-ups 71 → 80 overall; batsman share 50.5% → 52.8%. Users train harder on the same ~3 batsmen.
Lineup power shape unchanged (cohort): Lower2/Top2 ratio flat at 0.53, power CV flat at 0.27. Total batting power rises +10% but proportionally — all tiers grow together, not Lower2 catching up.
Gap widens slightly at cohort level: Top2−Lower2 gap 4,444 → 4,674 (+230). The typical user's lineup becomes more, not less, top-heavy.
Retire adopters (proxy, N=37) show a different signal: Lower2 mean +9% (+821), Top2−Lower2 gap closes by 6 points. Small sample, but directionally aligned with the Retire hypothesis.
70% roster overlap before→after: moderate churn (~1.2 new batsmen per user); not a wholesale team rebuild, so lineup changes are organic investment shifts.

Recommendation

One call

Cohort-level reads (both training and lineup) show no Retire-driven optimisation. The only directional signal is in the small Retire-proxy group. Next priority: build the actual Retire usage cohort (cumulative retire actions per user) and compare heavy Retire users vs zero-Retire users on Lower2 power and unique batsmen trained.
