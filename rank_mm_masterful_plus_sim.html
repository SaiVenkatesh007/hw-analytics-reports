Key findings

What the sim shows

Whale timer is the workhorse: Evening whales match at 100% under v1. ~38% of matches land in whale 0–10s; ~20% need rank…rank+2; only ~1% need 30s+ unlock.
v2 assists lift non-whales: Legendary 2 non-whale match 77%→95%; Legendary 1 81%→92%; M1 80%→88%. Expires concentrate in non-whales under v1.
Off-peak is the stress test: Hours 04–06 IST: v1 match 84–86% / expire 14–16%. v2 brings match to 92–93%. Peak hours already ~95% on v1.
Top-10 aversion is tiny today: ~1% of evening matches / ~215 tickets — bounds sit far below live power, so almost nobody is “top of band.”
Aspirational bounds: ~55% of Masterful+ flag as whale vs proposed uppers. Expect heavy whale-condition for 1–2 seasons post-launch until power redistributes.

Recommendation

One call

Ship same-rank + 0.9–1.1 + whale timed expansion, with cold-start / top-10 (v2). Use morning off-peak as the go/no-go lens for pool depth — not the evening deep-dive alone.

Simulation

How pools are built

Source: albatross_production.create · ARENAS · IS_USER
Engine: Tickets enter at create time. Every 5s, still-searching tickets with age < 60s form a concurrent pool. Greedy 1:1 (oldest first, power ratio closest to 1.0). Unmatched at 60s → expired.
Why not live run: Live runs are shaped by current MM leavers. Proposed rules may keep a ticket until run 3–4 — so we simulate our own runs from create.
Pool: pvpLeague ≥ 15 (Remarkable 3 filler for M1 cold-start). All outcome tables are Masterful+ (16–25).
